## Triad Pairs Concept (LH/RH Voicing)

### What Are Triad Pairs?
Triad pairs are two triads that don't share common tones, creating rich harmonic colors. In jazz piano, one triad is played in the left hand (LH) and another in the right hand (RH), creating sophisticated voicings.

### String Quartet Implementation
- **Cello (LH):** Lower triad (root, 3rd, 5th)
- **Viola + Violin II (RH):** Upper triad (root, 3rd, 5th)
- **Violin I:** Melody (can outline either triad or create counterpoint)

### Triad Pair Examples for G Major Context

**Example 1: G major + B minor**
- **Cello:** G-B-D (G major)
- **Viola/Violin II:** B-D-F# (B minor)
- **Result:** Gmaj7#11 (Lydian sound)
- **Use:** Measures 1-4, 41-44, 81-84

**Example 2: A minor + C major**
- **Cello:** A-C-E (A minor)
- **Viola/Violin II:** C-E-G (C major)
- **Result:** Am11 (sophisticated minor)
- **Use:** Measures 5-8, 45-48

**Example 3: D major + F# minor**
- **Cello:** D-F#-A (D major)
- **Viola/Violin II:** F#-A-C# (F# minor)
- **Result:** D9 (dominant with color)
- **Use:** Measures 9-12, 49-52

**Example 4: E minor + G major**
- **Cello:** E-G-B (E minor)
- **Viola/Violin II:** G-B-D (G major)
- **Result:** Em11 (modal minor)
- **Use:** Measures 21-24, 61-64

**Example 5: C major + E minor**
- **Cello:** C-E-G (C major)
- **Viola/Violin II:** E-G-B (E minor)
- **Result:** Cmaj9 (sophisticated major)
- **Use:** Measures 17-20, 57-60

**Example 6: B diminished + D major**
- **Cello:** B-D-F (B diminished)
- **Viola/Violin II:** D-F#-A (D major)
- **Result:** Bm7b5-D9 (tritone substitution color)
- **Use:** Measures 13-16, 53-56

## Polyrhythmic Patterns

### 3 Against 2 (Measures 41-44, 61-64)
- **Violin I:** Triplets (3 notes per beat)
- **Violin II/Viola/Cello:** Duplets (2 notes per beat)
- **Character:** Elegant, flowing, sophisticated

### 4 Against 3 (Measures 45-48, 73-76)
- **Violin I:** 16th notes (4 notes per beat)
- **Violin II/Viola/Cello:** Triplets (3 notes per beat)
- **Character:** Complex, sophisticated, air-like

### 5 Against 4 (Measures 49-52)
- **Violin I:** Quintuplets (5 notes per beat)
- **Violin II/Viola/Cello:** 16th notes (4 notes per beat)
- **Character:** Very sophisticated, modern, air-like

## Régine Crespin / Aretha Franklin Duet Character

### Vocal Character Translation to String Quartet

**Régine Crespin (Opera):**
- **Violin I:** Operatic, soaring, elegant melodies
- **Character:** Long, legato lines, expressive dynamics
- **Register:** Upper register, sustained notes
- **Technique:** Portamento, vibrato, expressive phrasing

**Aretha Franklin (Soul/R&B):**
- **Violin II:** Soulful, expressive countermelodies
- **Character:** Rhythmic, syncopated, emotional
- **Register:** Mid-upper register, melodic lines
- **Technique:** Slides, expressive articulations, rhythmic drive

**Duet Interaction:**
- **Measures 1-20:** Violin I (Crespin) leads, Violin II (Franklin) responds
- **Measures 21-40:** Violin II (Franklin) leads, Violin I (Crespin) responds
- **Measures 41-60:** Both voices intertwine (duet)
- **Measures 61-80:** Call and response between voices
- **Measures 81-90:** Both voices unite in final resolution

### Expressive Elements

**Operatic (Crespin):**
- Long, sustained notes
- Expressive crescendos/diminuendos
- Portamento (slides between notes)
- Vibrato
- Dynamic range: `p` → `f` → `p`

**Soulful (Franklin):**
- Rhythmic syncopation
- Expressive slides
- Dynamic accents
- Rhythmic drive
- Emotional intensity

**Duet Moments:**
- **Measures 41-44:** Both voices in polyrhythm (3 against 2)
- **Measures 49-52:** Both voices in complex polyrhythm (5 against 4)
- **Measures 65-68:** Call and response
- **Measures 81-90:** Unison and harmony
